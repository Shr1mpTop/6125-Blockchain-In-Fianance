Idea 5: Container Roll-over Micro-insurance for SMEs——LI JIAWEI
1) Problem

In maritime shipping and supply chain finance, small and medium-sized enterprises often suffer from container roll-over, meaning that their booked cargo is not loaded onto the planned vessel and must wait for a later sailing. This may happen because of vessel overbooking, port congestion, capacity shortages, schedule disruption, or priority given to larger customers. For SMEs, even a short delay can create serious financial pressure. Goods may miss delivery deadlines, buyers may impose penalties, production schedules may be interrupted, and cash flow may be delayed.

However, traditional insurance products are usually not well suited to this problem. The loss caused by one roll-over event may be relatively small compared with large marine cargo claims, but the verification process is still costly. Insurers need to check shipping documents, carrier notices, port records, delay evidence, and the actual financial impact. For micro-level claims, the administrative cost may be higher than the premium or compensation amount. As a result, many SMEs remain underinsured, even though they are highly vulnerable to shipping disruptions.

2) Blockchain Solution

A blockchain-based parametric micro-insurance model can make container roll-over insurance economically feasible. Instead of requiring manual claim assessment, the policy can be written into a smart contract. The insured SME pays a small premium before shipment. The smart contract defines clear trigger conditions, such as: the container was booked on Vessel A, but official carrier or port data confirms that it was not loaded and was postponed to a later vessel.

Once the trigger is verified through trusted data sources, such as carrier APIs, port operation systems, electronic bills of lading, or logistics platforms, the smart contract automatically releases a fixed payout to the SME. This removes the need for lengthy claim submission and manual review. Blockchain also provides a transparent and tamper-resistant record of the insurance policy, shipment status, trigger data, and payout history.

The key advantage is that smart contracts reduce marginal claim-processing costs close to zero. This allows insurers or decentralized insurance pools to offer low-premium, high-frequency micro-insurance products that traditional insurers would find uneconomical. For SMEs, the solution provides faster compensation, better cash-flow protection, and greater resilience against unpredictable maritime supply chain disruptions.
