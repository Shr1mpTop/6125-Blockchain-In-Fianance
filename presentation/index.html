1 · Problem definition

The SME liquidity trap

For Singapore SME importers moving pharmaceuticals, fresh produce, and premium food, maritime disruption is not just a logistics problem. It freezes working capital inside cargo that cannot be sold, delivered, or financed on schedule.

Traditional claims arrive after the cash crisis.

OceanNode reframes the problem: protect the SME cash cycle at the moment a verified physical event breaks it.

3.4.1 · Underwriting capital logic

LP capital is programmable insurance capacity

The pool does not simply hold deposits. It moves LP capital through explicit states so OceanNode cannot sell coverage without reserved payout capacity.

01

Deposited / Available

LP stakes USDC and receives pool shares.

02

Reserved

Policy issuance locks capital as underwriting capacity.

03

Premium-Earning

SME premium is routed into the pool as yield.

04

Claimed

A valid roll-over or port-delay trigger pays the SME.

05

Released

No trigger before expiry returns capacity to the pool.

06

Withdrawable

LP exits with available USDC and earned yield, minus claims.

4 · Digital asset comparison

Stable settlement, not speculative exposure

OceanNode uses USDC for premiums, reserves, and payouts because SMEs need claim value that behaves like working capital, while LPs need a stable unit of account for loss ratios and yield.

USDC / stablecoin Best prototype fit

Fast settlement, low volatility, direct smart contract composability.

CBDC Institutional future

Strong trust profile, but not yet practical for an open DeFi-style pool.

ETH / volatile crypto Rejected for payouts

Adds market risk on top of maritime underwriting risk.

$NODE Optional incentives only

Useful for governance, not for claim-paying capacity.

5.1 · Evaluation risks

The design is only credible if the risks are explicit

Oracle risk

Cross-check AIS feeds with port and terminal records before confirming payout.

Pool solvency

Cap insured exposure per pool and reserve payout capital before policies go live.

Stablecoin risk

Whitelist reputable backed stablecoins and disclose issuer, freeze, and depeg risk.

Regulatory risk

Use KYC/AML controls and partner with licensed insurers for enforceable policies.

5.2 · Evaluation & valuation

From Web3 proof-of-concept to regulated FinTech infrastructure

OceanNode is most viable as a hybrid B2B/B2B2C platform: licensed insurers legally issue policies, while blockchain infrastructure handles decentralized underwriting, oracle-triggered automation, and atomic settlement.
